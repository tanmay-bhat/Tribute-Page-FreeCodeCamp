Richard Stallman

The man who started GNU & FOSS

Stallman giving a speech on "Free Software and Your Freedom" at the biennale du design of Saint-Étienne (2008)

    Life of Richard Stallman :

  • Stallman announced the plan for the GNU operating system in September 1983
  • In 1985, Stallman published the GNU Manifesto, which outlined his motivation for creating a free operating system called GNU
  • In 1989 the first program-independent GNU General Public License (GPL) was released.
  • Stallman was responsible for contributing text editor (Emacs), compiler (GCC), debugger (GNU Debugger)
  • Stallman's influences on hacker culture include the name POSIX and the Emacs editor.
  • Linus Torvalds has criticized Stallman for what he considers "black-and-white thinking".
  • In 1999, Stallman called for development of a free online encyclopedia called GNUPedia.
  • Stallman is a world traveler and has visited at least 65 countries, mostly to speak about free software and the GNU project.
  • In August 2006, at his meetings with the government of the Indian State of Kerala, he told officials to discard proprietary software, such as Microsoft's, at state-run schools. Resulted in a landmark decision to switch all school computers in 12,500 high schools from Windows to a free software operating system.
  • Protesting against proprietary software in April 2006, Stallman held a "Don't buy from ATI, enemy of your freedom" .
  • Stallman professes admiration for whistleblowers Julian Assange[92] and Edward Snowden
  • Stallman has said that he is "an atheist of Jewish ancestry"[8] and often wears a button that reads "Impeach God".
  • He recieved awards like in 1986: Honorary lifetime membership of the Chalmers University of Technology Computer Society
  • 1998: Electronic Frontier Foundation's Pioneer award[1998: Electronic Frontier Foundation's Pioneer award.
  • 2012: Honorary professorship, from the Universidad César Vallejo de Trujillo, in Peru.
